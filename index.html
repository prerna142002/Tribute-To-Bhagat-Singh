Bhagat Singh

September 1907-23 March 1931

Indian Socialist Revolutionary

  • 1921

    Became disillusioned with Mahatma Gandhi's philosophy of non-violence after he called off the non-co-operation movement.

  • 1922

    Gandhi's decision followed the violent murders of policemen by villagers who were reacting to the police killing three villagers in the 1922 Chauri Chaura incident. Singh joined the Young Revolutionary Movement and began to advocate for the violent overthrow of the British Government in India.

  • 1923

    Won an essay competition set by the Punjab Hindi Sahitya Sammelan, writing on the problems in the Punjab.
    joined the National College in Lahore, where he also participated in extra-curricular activities like the dramatics society.

  • 1926

    Inspired by the Young Italy movement of Giuseppe Mazzini, he founded the Indian nationalist youth organisation Naujawan Bharat Sabha in March 1926. He also joined the Hindustan Republican Association, which had prominent leaders, such as Chandrashekhar Azad, Ram Prasad Bismil and Shahid Ashfaqallah Khan.

  • 1927

    Police became concerned with Singh's influence on youths and arrested him in May 1927 on the pretext that he had been involved in a bombing that had taken place in Lahore in October 1926.

  • 1928

    Prominent member of the HRA and was probably responsible, in large part, for its change of name to HSRA in 1928. The HSRA vowed to avenge Rai's death.   • 1929

    Proposed a dramatic act to the HSRA intended to gain massive publicity for their aims.
    On 8 April 1929, Singh, accompanied by Batukeshwar Dutt, threw two bombs into the Assembly chamber from its public gallery while it was in session. The bombs had been designed not to kill, but some members, including George Ernest Schuster, the finance member of the Viceroy's Executive Council, were injured. The smoke from the bombs filled the Assembly so that Singh and Dutt could probably have escaped in the confusion had they wished. Instead, they stayed shouting the slogan "Inquilab Zindabad!" ("Long Live the Revolution") and threw leaflets. The two men were arrested and subsequently moved through a series of jails in Delhi.
    After they were arrested, they witnessed discrimination between European and Indian prisoners. Singh led other prisoners in a hunger strike to protest this. They demanded equality in standards of food, clothing , toiletries, and other hygienic necessities.

  • 1931

    Bhagat singh , Rajguru and Sukhdev were hanged on 27th March, for Lahore Conspiracy Case.
